# End-to-End Digitization of SBL & AHL Loan Journeys

## Overview

At AU Small Finance Bank (formerly Fincare), I led a large-scale digital transformation initiative focused on streamlining the end-to-end Affordable Housing Loan (AHL) and Small Business Loan (SBL) customer journeys.

The business operated on two independent CRM systems with no integration between them. Lead management, customer processing, credit reviews, approvals, reporting, and channel tracking were fragmented across systems and teams. This resulted in duplicate effort, poor visibility, inaccurate reporting, extended turnaround times, and a poor customer experience.

While managing Connector, Insurance, and Strategic Alliance channels, I identified that sustainable growth would require fixing the core lending workflow first. I therefore took ownership of the initiative and partnered with Product, Technology, Operations, Credit, Legal, Finance, Marketing, and Business teams to redesign and digitize the lending journey.

---

## The Challenge

Several operational and technology challenges existed:

- Two independent CRM systems operated without integration.
- Customer and lead information was maintained in different formats across systems.
- Credit rejections and pending cases often had to be restarted as fresh applications.
- Historical customer interactions and correction notes were not visible across teams.
- Duplicate effort increased turnaround times and operational workload.
- Leads from websites, branches, connectors, alliances, and other channels were difficult to track consistently.
- Ownership of leads was often unclear, creating disputes and inefficiencies.
- Management reporting required extensive manual effort and lacked accuracy.

As a result, exception cases often remained unresolved for more than 90 days and customer journeys became increasingly fragmented.

---

## My Approach

### 1. Process Mapping & Gap Analysis

I conducted detailed process mapping across:

- Lead management
- Credit review workflows
- Approval journeys
- Insurance processing
- Channel onboarding
- Customer communication
- MIS reporting

This helped identify critical bottlenecks and duplicate processes across systems.

### 2. Redesigning the Lending Workflow

Working closely with stakeholders, I redesigned the customer journey to include:

- Digital lead capture and upload.
- Automated first-level filtering of non-eligible cases.
- Credit review notes and correction tracking.
- Structured customer communication workflows.
- Automated SMS and status updates.
- Approval workflow standardization.
- Insurance process automation.
- Geography, branch, RM, and employee mapping.
- Centralized data storage and reporting.

This reduced dependency on manual intervention and improved visibility throughout the customer lifecycle.

### 3. Driving Technology Alignment

One of the biggest challenges was aligning two independent IT teams managing different CRM platforms.

API integration was initially not prioritized because of competing technology projects and differences in data structures across systems.

To move the initiative forward, I:

- Presented the business impact to senior leadership.
- Secured executive sponsorship and prioritization.
- Coordinated weekly governance meetings.
- Created detailed BRDs and functional requirements.
- Facilitated discussions between both technology teams.
- Established dedicated ownership for project execution.
- Conducted phased testing and validation before rollout.

Implementations were carefully scheduled to avoid disruption to business operations and month-end activities.

### 4. Rollout & Change Management

To ensure successful adoption, I led:

- UAT coordination.
- Branch-level testing.
- Training sessions.
- Process documentation.
- Cross-functional stakeholder communication.
- Rollout monitoring and feedback collection.

---

## Outcomes

### Business Impact

- Reduced exception-case turnaround time from more than 90 days to approximately 15–20 days.
- Eliminated duplicate application processing for correction cases.
- Improved customer visibility throughout the lending lifecycle.
- Reduced manual effort across branch, operations, and credit teams.
- Increased accuracy of lead ownership and channel attribution.
- Improved reporting quality and business decision-making through automated MIS.

### Operational Impact

- Established a unified process across multiple acquisition channels.
- Eliminated data fallouts and tracking gaps.
- Improved coordination between Business, Operations, Credit, and Technology teams.
- Simplified integration of Connector, Insurance, and Strategic Alliance channels into the lending journey.

### Organizational Impact

- Created a scalable lending workflow that supported PAN India operations.
- Standardized customer processing and tracking mechanisms.
- Introduced several process enhancements that were later incorporated into AU's CRM ecosystem during the Fincare–AU merger.
- Improved readiness for future automation and digital transformation initiatives.

---

## Leadership Impact

One of the most challenging aspects of the project was aligning Technology and Operations teams around a common vision.

Technology teams were hesitant due to competing priorities and system complexities, while Operations teams were concerned about disruption to existing processes.

Through continuous stakeholder management, executive alignment, governance reviews, training programs, and phased implementation, I successfully drove adoption across functions and ensured long-term sustainability of the solution.
