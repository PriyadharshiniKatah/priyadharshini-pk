# Connector Channel Transformation: Building a Scalable Acquisition Engine

## Overview

At AU Small Finance Bank, I owned the Connector Channel for Affordable Housing Loans (AHL) and Loan Against Property (LAP) products.

Connectors are third-party individuals or organizations that source and refer potential customers to the bank. They play a critical role in expanding market reach and generating qualified loan opportunities.

When I took over the channel, more than 3,200 connectors had been onboarded historically, but fewer than 1,500 were actively contributing business. The channel lacked structure, digitization, governance, and engagement mechanisms, resulting in low productivity, partner dissatisfaction, and limited business contribution.

Over a 3.5-year period, I led the transformation of the channel into a scalable acquisition engine that significantly increased partner participation, business contribution, and operational efficiency.

---

## The Challenge

The channel faced multiple operational and growth challenges:

- Less than 50% of onboarded connectors were active.
- No structured engagement or retention framework existed.
- Incentive payouts were delayed, leading to dissatisfaction and attrition.
- The ecosystem consisted primarily of individual connectors with limited participation from larger connector organizations.
- Manual processes required connectors to physically visit branches for submissions and updates.
- No dedicated digital platform existed for onboarding, lead tracking, or business monitoring.
- Poor documentation quality led to record fallouts and repeated follow-ups.
- Branch teams spent significant effort reviewing incomplete or low-quality applications.

As a result, the channel contributed only a small share of overall business despite its significant growth potential.

---

## My Approach

### 1. Expanding the Connector Ecosystem

I developed a multi-pronged acquisition strategy focused on expanding both individual and institutional connector networks.

Key initiatives included:

- Partnering with large broker groups and connector organizations.
- Running referral programs for customers, employees, and existing connectors.
- Building presence within connector communities across social media platforms.
- Leveraging digital advertising campaigns for connector acquisition.
- Launching activation and retention campaigns for dormant connectors.

### 2. Creating Structured Partner Programs

To improve engagement and retention, I introduced:

- Performance-based incentive structures.
- Connector contests and recognition programs.
- Activation campaigns targeting inactive partners.
- Regular business reviews and communication frameworks.

These initiatives helped build long-term engagement while improving productivity.

### 3. Digitizing the Connector Journey

One of the most significant initiatives was the introduction of a dedicated Connector App.

Working closely with Technology, Legal, Finance, and Operations teams, I helped digitize key parts of the connector journey, including:

- Lead submission.
- Document management.
- Incentive tracking.
- Status updates.
- Performance monitoring.

This reduced dependency on branch visits and improved the overall partner experience.

### 4. Operational Excellence & Automation

I implemented several process improvements, including:

- Automated MIS reporting.
- Geography-wise performance tracking.
- Structured performance dashboards.
- Mandatory validation checkpoints before applications reached branch teams.
- T+2 incentive payout mechanisms.
- Workflow automation to reduce manual intervention.

These changes improved visibility, reduced operational friction, and allowed branch teams to focus on high-quality opportunities.

---

## Outcomes

### Business Impact

- Increased active connector base from fewer than 1,500 to more than 26,000.
- Improved channel penetration from 32% to 69%.
- Significantly increased channel contribution to overall disbursement volumes.
- Established the Connector Channel as a major acquisition source across products.
- Successfully scaled the model across additional businesses including LAG and Microfinance.

### Operational Impact

- Reduced reporting effort through automation and dashboarding.
- Improved documentation quality and lead qualification.
- Reduced branch effort spent on low-quality applications.
- Improved turnaround times through digital workflows and automated processes.

### Organizational Impact

- Built a scalable and repeatable acquisition model.
- Strengthened collaboration across Business, Technology, Operations, Legal, Finance, and Product teams.
- Created a framework that could be replicated across multiple business lines.

### Leadership Impact

The success of the transformation led business leaders from other product lines, including LAG and Microfinance, to engage me in revamping their own connector ecosystems.

I also led connector-channel continuity and integration efforts during the Fincare–AU merger, helping establish and scale a channel that did not previously exist within AU's ecosystem.
