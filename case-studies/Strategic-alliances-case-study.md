# Strategic Alliances: Building a New Acquisition Channel from Scratch

## Overview

At AU Small Finance Bank (formerly Fincare), I was selected by the leadership team of the Affordable Housing Loan (AHL) and Loan Against Property (LAP) businesses to build and scale a completely new Strategic Alliances channel.

The initiative started from zero. There was no existing process, operating model, budget allocation, technology framework, partner ecosystem, or success metrics. The objective was to create an alternative acquisition channel that could deliver high-quality customers, expand market reach, and improve profitability.

Over time, I established the channel as a permanent acquisition engine by building partnerships, designing processes, driving technology integrations, and creating governance structures that enabled sustainable growth.

---

## The Challenge

Building the channel presented several challenges:

- No existing alliance framework or operating model.
- No approved budget or defined success metrics.
- Uncertainty around ROI of technology and API integrations.
- Legal, compliance, audit, and documentation requirements needed to be established from scratch.
- Internal teams had limited understanding of alliance-led acquisition models.
- Prospective partners were hesitant due to uncertainty around commercials, integrations, and operational processes.
- Multiple discussions and negotiations were required to align commission structures, payouts, and responsibilities.

The challenge was not simply acquiring partners, but creating an entirely new business channel within the organization.

---

## My Approach

### 1. Defining the Alliance Strategy

I structured the channel into two distinct partnership models:

#### Lead Generation Partnerships

Partnered with lead-generation and financial marketplace platforms including:

- Paisabazaar
- CreditMantri
- Airtel Payments Bank
- India Post Payments Bank (IPPB)

Under this model, partners supplied qualified customer leads and commercial structures were designed around lead purchases and conversion-based payouts.

#### Ecosystem Partnerships

Established strategic partnerships with builders, construction companies, and housing ecosystem players including organizations such as:

- Shankara
- Upkar

These partnerships enabled direct access to customers actively seeking housing finance solutions.

---

### 2. Building the Partner Ecosystem

I identified and sourced potential partners through:

- Market research
- LinkedIn outreach
- Industry networking
- Business conventions and events
- Ecosystem mapping
- Strategic GTM discussions

I personally managed the complete partner lifecycle from identification and onboarding to commercial negotiations and post-launch governance.

---

### 3. Creating the Operating Model

As the channel owner, I designed and implemented:

- Partner onboarding frameworks
- Commercial and commission structures
- Governance mechanisms
- Reporting and MIS frameworks
- Performance review processes
- Lead management workflows

This created a scalable foundation for future growth.

---

### 4. Technology & Process Enablement

Working closely with Technology, Compliance, Finance, Marketing, Legal, and Branch teams, I led:

- API integration discussions
- CRM enhancements
- New workflow design
- MIS automation
- Partner performance tracking
- Operational process standardization

These capabilities enabled efficient lead management and improved conversion outcomes.

## Outcomes

- Successfully established a completely new acquisition channel from zero.
- Onboarded 7 alliance partners and 2 Top-Up Loan partners at Home First Finance.

- Achieved approximately 13% lead-to-customer conversion.
- Delivered 17% profitability growth through higher-quality customer acquisition and improved conversion economics.
- Expanded customer reach beyond traditional acquisition channels.

- Created a permanent and scalable acquisition channel.
- Improved access to pre-qualified customer segments.

### Organizational Impact
